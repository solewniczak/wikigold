A giant black [[Bear|bear|1.0]], whom [[Peasant|peasants|0.4]] have nicknamed ``Dracula,'' is creating anxiety in western [[Romania]] because of his taste for cattle and [[Domestic sheep|sheep|0.2]], an official said Thursday.

Peasants whose [[Livestock|livestock|0.4]] has been devoured by the hungry bear have lodged seven complaints in the past six weeks, said Nicolae Onetiu, head of the state hunting and fishing [[Game reserve|reserves|0.2]] in western Romania.

[[Dracula]] was named after the [[Transylvania|Transylvania|0.8]]n [[vampire]] count of legend because of his bloody appetite, said Onetiu. The male bruin weighs at least 300 [[Kilogram|kilograms|0.2]] (660 pounds) and has moved closer to inhabited areas from forested regions with the onset of cold weather, but is apparently not yet ready to [[Hibernation|hibernate|0.8]].

Dracula has killed at least six cows and seven sheep over the past six weeks.

Most bears are [[Omnivore|omnivorous|0.6]], but in Romania, many bears were [[Breastfeeding|weaned|0.2]] on meat, said Onetiu. [[Nicolae Ceau≈üescu|Nicolae Ceausescu]], the [[Communism|communist|0.2]] [[Dictator|dictator|0.2]] toppled and executed almost a decade ago, was an avid hunter, and hunting reserve cubs were fed meat to make them grow bigger quicker, leaving generations partial to meat.

State [[Park ranger|rangers|0.2]] count 135 bears living in the [[Apuseni Mountains]] in western Romania, all of them protected by law.
